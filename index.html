Hidden Risks Under Pressure

Machine Learning for Early Risk Stratification of Multidrug-Resistant Wound Infections in Conflict-Related Trauma Care

I want to conduct a research project at the intersection of machine learning, medicine, and war-related public health, focusing on whether machine learning could help identify patients at higher risk of multidrug-resistant wound infections before complete microbiological results are available.

The Problem

Antimicrobial resistance is one of the major global challenges facing medicine. In war-related trauma care, the problem becomes even more urgent because injuries are often complex, hospitals can be overloaded, and doctors may need to make decisions before complete microbiological results are available. Multidrug-resistant wound infections can make treatment slower, more complicated, and less effective.

Why This Matters in My Community

In Ukraine, many people understand the immediate danger of conflict-related injuries, but fewer think about infection risks that can appear later and make recovery more difficult. This project focuses on a less visible medical consequence of war: resistant wound infections in overloaded healthcare systems.

?
Can machine learning help identify patients at higher risk of multidrug-resistant wound infections in conflict-related trauma care before complete microbiological results are available?

My Research Approach

Literature Review

Study antimicrobial resistance, wound infections, and risk factors in trauma care.

Conceptual Framework

Identify possible risk factors such as wound type, time before treatment, previous antibiotic use, trauma complexity, and laboratory indicators.

Python Prototype

If suitable public data is available, build a simple classifier and compare models such as logistic regression, decision trees, and random forests.

How I Would Evaluate It

I would evaluate the prototype using precision, recall, and false negatives. Recall and false negatives would be especially important because missing a high-risk case could be the most dangerous outcome.

Responsible Use and Limits

This project would not be a clinical diagnostic tool. It would not prescribe antibiotics, replace doctors, or make independent medical conclusions. It would be a research-based prototype and framework, developed with guidance from a mentor in medical informatics, public health, or computational medicine.

Expected Deliverables

  • Literature review
  • Conceptual framework for early MDR wound infection risk stratification
  • Python prototype, if data allows
  • Model comparison and evaluation
  • Clear limitations and ethical boundaries

Potential Impact

The goal is to explore how data-driven tools could support earlier risk awareness, laboratory prioritization, and infection-control decisions in overloaded healthcare systems. For me, this project reflects the kind of computer science I want to build: technology that does not replace human judgment, but helps reveal hidden risks earlier and supports better decisions when delays can have serious human consequences.
